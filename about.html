About Me

Bio

I've always had an interest in computers. Going onto our old family computer and playing Solitaire and Pinball got me addicted. I was around 11 years old when I was enrolled in a Junior Digital Media bootcamp which taught me basic HTML. Whilst others followed the teacher and only worked on it at class, I kept on working on it at home, looking up cool things to add.

Fast forward to today, and I love being able to create things from scratch that can simplify a task. Python projects are a favourite of mine, however I do enjoy creating responsive websites that are visually appealing.

Experience

My first job was working as a nightfill staff member for a local supermarket whilst in high school. After graduating high school, I took multiple customer service jobs over the next 12 or so years, learning how to critically think and problem solve. I truly believe these experiences have helped me to become a better developer.

Now that I've mastered the art of complaint resolution, it's definitely time to start the career I've always wanted to have. Currently studying a Bachelor of Information Technology (Computer Science) at the Queensland University of Technology, I'm excited for what's to come and the knowledge I'll gain.

Interests

Apart from all things tech-related, I'm a massive sports fan, frequently watching the NRL as an avid supporter of the Brisbane Broncos. I also played a lot of volleyball when I was younger and helped create an amateur sports club that is still operating now. I like to play guitar and ukulele as well

Traveling and exploring are two hand-in-hand activities that I plan to do a lot more of. Having already visited Indonesia and the Philippines, there are so many more places on my bucket list. I have a fascination with learning languages too - Indonesian and Spanish are beginner levels whilst I'm still learning Tagalog.
